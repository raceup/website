Aerodynamics

The aerodynamics department deals with the study of the aerodynamic package consisting of lateral, bottom, front and rear wing, with the aim of increasing grip and reducing the vehicle's drag. At the design stage, through fluid dynamics simulations, we seek the best compromise between grip and efficiency, in particular for electric cars where consumption is a limit. Components for both machines are designed and made of carbon fiber to reduce masses and optimize performance.

Frame

The chassis department designs and carries out the car frame structure, working closely with the suspension and drive-train teams to ensure that all components are positioned and supported properly. In addition to the seat and steering production, we have made ergonomic studies for the driver's driving position. As for the combustion car, our goal is to equip the MG with a rigid chassis (to ensure suspension-designed trim), durable (to withstand loads on track) and lightweight (to ensure high performance ). The tubular frame 2017 is the result of more than 10 years of field experience that has allowed our department to optimize design and performance while seeking continuous improvement.

Brakes and suspensions

The suspension team deals with the study of the dynamics of the vehicle, the structural dimensioning of most mechanical components in the vehicle and the calculation of the braking system. The team's guidelines are to lower the weight, lower the center of mass, and control the weight distribution. To do this, the dynamics and kinematics of the vehicle in acceleration / braking and curve are first studied, starting from the tire data up to the choice of suspension geometry and shock absorbers to achieve the desired behavior. It then deals with the structural dimensioning of mechanical components such as hubs, triangles, rockers and various supports that connect suspensions to the body, and calculates the braking system. For the electric car, 10-inch wheels, Hoosier 17 x 8.5 tires and double triangular push-road suspensions have been chosen.

Engine and drivetrain

The Engine & Drivetrain department deals with several aspects that can be summarized in four macro domains: fluid dynamics, transmission, cooling and auxiliary systems. In the first field, the main goal is to increase the power and torque that can be expressed by the engine, currently a Honda CBR 600 RR. In particular, the philosophy on which the engine suction and exhaust systems and timing systems were focused was to obtain a constant torque when changing the rotation speed to maximize engine performance. The task of transmitting the torque produced to the wheels, from the crankshaft inside the engine itself to the semi-axle, is under the Transmission team, trying to maximize mechanical performance and contain the weights. The most affected parts are gearbox, sprocket and crown, self-locking differential. As of the cooling, you manage the water and oil temperatures to maximize performance without compromising reliability. Finally, they are part of the Auxiliary Systems, indispensable components such as those of the gasoline line and gearbox and clutch implement systems.

Powertrain

The powertrain team deals with everything that makes up the propulsion system of the car: electric motors, transmission, inverter and battery. The motors are directly connected to the transmission and mounted inside the wheels, a very critical position because of its influence on the dynamics of the vehicle. That's why they have been designed and built by the team to maximize their performance. Similar argument applies to self-developed inverters, lighter than any commercial and battery option, subject to stringent rules regarding real-time monitoring and security systems.

Elettronics

The electronic team acts as a support to other departments and works with them to ensure that each part of the car works perfectly, by interconnecting all its electronic subsystems. To do this, we design and realize a wiring that is as light as possible, connecting every single sensor with over 20 inputs from electronic and mechanical components. The data thus acquired, through control algorithms inside the Control Unit, that is developed internally in the car, are designed and run functions such as Traction Control in both vehicles, Torque Vectoring, exclusive of the electrical and Launch Control for the combustion. This ensures stability and performance of the car in order to maximize its performance. Other components are telemetry and data loggers, in the electric car, that allow you to view and save information on the car's operation. In addition, specifically for the electric car, the Battery Management System, also self-developed, monitors battery status in real time, ensuring that power is delivered safely. Finally, we design and build safety systems that can turn off the car in case of problems.
